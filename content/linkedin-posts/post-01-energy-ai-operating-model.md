# LinkedIn Authority Post

## Title
Most Energy Companies Aren't Failing at AI Because They Lack Tools

## Source Memo
Houston Energy AI Operating Model

## Industry
Energy

## Post
Most energy companies aren't failing at AI because they lack tools.

They're failing because they adopted AI without an operating model.

Across Houston I'm seeing the same pattern:

• Teams experimenting with tools
• Vendors pitching "AI solutions"
• Pilots running inside isolated departments

But no clear answer to a much more important question:

Where does AI actually belong in the operating model of an energy company?

Energy infrastructure is not a SaaS startup.

It operates inside:

• regulatory oversight
• safety constraints
• capital-intensive systems
• mission-critical uptime requirements

That means AI adoption cannot be treated like a software rollout.

It has to be treated like operational infrastructure.

The companies that win in energy AI will not be the ones that deploy tools the fastest.

They will be the ones that design governance, workflows, and ROI models before deployment begins.

Speed without structure creates risk.

Structure creates leverage.

Houston has the opportunity to lead here.

# Command: Run Phase 2 Week

Execute the Phase 2 activation loop for one standard week (3 posts).
Authoritative runbook: `docs/phase-2-activation-runbook.md`

---

## Preflight (Before Monday)

- [ ] Open `content/linkedin-posts/` and confirm at least 3 posts are ready to publish.
      If the queue is short, draft new posts from `content/executive-memos/` before proceeding.
- [ ] Select the 3 posts for this week. Note the file name of each.
- [ ] Decide hook usage: apply the Office Hours hook to 1 of the 3 posts (~33%).
      Review `templates/office-hours-hook.md` and select a variant (A, B, or C).
      Confirm the chosen post type is appropriate for the hook (framework, challenge framing,
      or contrast post — not an observational or question-close post).

---

## Monday

**Publish Post 1**

1. Open the first selected post from `content/linkedin-posts/`.
2. Copy the post body into LinkedIn. Publish.
3. If this post carries the hook: append the selected variant from
   `templates/office-hours-hook.md` below the final paragraph, preceded by a blank line.
4. Log the post title and publish date in your weekly note.
5. Review any signals from the prior weekend (see Daily Signal Review below).

---

## Wednesday

**Publish Post 2**

1. Open the second selected post from `content/linkedin-posts/`.
2. Copy and publish. Apply hook if this post was designated for it.
3. Log the publish.

---

## Friday

**Publish Post 3**

1. Open the third selected post from `content/linkedin-posts/`.
2. Copy and publish. Apply hook if designated.
3. Log the publish.
4. Run the weekly retrospective (see below).

---

## Daily Signal Review (10 Minutes, Every Business Day)

**Step 1 — Check LinkedIn**
- Review notifications on all posts published in the last 7 days.
- Check DMs for any replies or unsolicited outreach.

**Step 2 — Identify signals**
Count the following as signals worth logging:
- Substantive comments (not just emoji or "great post")
- Shares with added context
- Direct messages referencing a post
- Connection requests from relevant roles (Director, VP, C-Suite, Owner)
- Referrals or introductions

A like alone is a weak signal (score 1–2). Log only if the person's role or company
is relevant.

**Step 3 — Log in CRM tracker**
For each qualifying signal, open your working copy of `templates/crm-signal-tracker.md`
and complete a new entry:
- Date, contact name, company, role, industry
- Source post, signal type, signal strength (1–5)
- Notes on what was said or implied
- Next action and follow-up date (if known)
- Status: set to `New` or `Reviewed`

Keep this step under 10 minutes. Flag signals that need a thoughtful response;
address those during engagement review later in the day.

---

## Hook Usage Rules (Reference)

Full rules: `templates/office-hours-hook.md`

**Use the hook on:**
- Posts framing a specific organizational challenge
- Posts presenting a framework or decision model
- Posts drawing a contrast between two approaches

**Do not use the hook on:**
- Observational or data-only posts
- Short-form takes or single-insight posts
- Posts that already end with a direct question to the audience

Use one variant per post. Do not repeat the same variant in consecutive posts.
Target: 1 hook per standard week (30–40% of posts).

---

## Friday Retrospective Checklist

Run after publishing Post 3.

- [ ] Were 3 posts published this week?
- [ ] Were all signals logged in the CRM tracker within 24 hours?
- [ ] Did any inbound interest go unaddressed for more than 48 hours?
- [ ] Was the Office Hours hook used on approximately one post?
- [ ] Are there open CRM entries with status `New` that need a next action assigned?
- [ ] Does the post queue in `content/linkedin-posts/` have at least 3 posts ready for next week?

**Queue Refill (if needed)**
If the queue has fewer than 3 ready posts, draft new ones before closing the week.
Source from `content/executive-memos/`. Use the format in `templates/linkedin-post-template.md`
if available, or match the style of existing posts in `content/linkedin-posts/`.

# LinkedIn Authority Post

## Title
The Biggest Risk of AI in the Energy Industry Isn't Automation

## Source Memo
Houston Energy AI Operating Model

## Industry
Energy

## Post
The biggest risk of AI in the energy industry isn't automation.

It's fragmentation.

Right now many companies are experimenting with AI in pockets:

• engineering teams testing copilots
• analysts using AI for reporting
• operations teams exploring predictive models

Individually these experiments look promising.

But collectively they create something dangerous.

A fragmented AI environment where:

• different teams use different models
• data governance becomes unclear
• outputs can't be trusted consistently

Energy systems cannot operate this way.

Consistency is part of the operating model.

The leaders I'm seeing succeed with AI in Houston are doing something different:

They're starting with governance frameworks, not tools.

They define:

• who owns AI decisions
• how models interact with operational data
• how results are validated

Then they deploy AI inside those boundaries.

AI in energy isn't just about capability.

It's about operational trust.

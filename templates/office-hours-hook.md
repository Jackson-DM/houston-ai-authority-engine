# Office Hours Hook — LinkedIn Append Templates

Three variants for inviting professional conversation at the close of a post or in follow-up.
Select one per post. Do not stack variants or modify the tone toward urgency.

---

## Variant A — Open Invitation

```
If this surfaces a question worth talking through,
I hold a standing block for conversations like this.
No agenda, no pitch — just the work.
Reply here or reach out directly.
```

---

## Variant B — Peer Context

```
I work through these questions regularly with operators
navigating similar decisions in their organizations.
If it would be useful, I'm available for a direct conversation.
```

---

## Variant C — Minimal Close

```
Happy to talk through how this applies to your context.
I keep time reserved for that kind of conversation.
```

---

## Usage

**End of post:**
Append Variant A or B below the final paragraph of a LinkedIn post, separated by a blank line.
Use sparingly — no more than one in three posts should carry an explicit invitation.

**Comments:**
Use Variant C as a reply to substantive comments that indicate a real organizational challenge.
Keep it brief; the comment is not a pitch.

**DM follow-up:**
When someone likes or shares a post without commenting, Variant B works as a low-pressure
opening line in a direct message. Pair it with a single specific observation about their work
or company before including the variant text.
